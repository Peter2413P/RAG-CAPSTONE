UNIVERSITY ACADEMIC REGULATIONS HANDBOOK 2024-2025

CHAPTER 1: EXAMINATION RULES AND PROCEDURES

1.1 General Examination Rules
All students must carry their valid university identity card to every examination hall. Students who fail to produce their ID card will not be permitted to sit the examination. Examinations are held twice a year: End Semester Examination (ESE) in November-December and April-May.

1.2 Attendance Requirements
A minimum attendance of 75% is mandatory in each subject. Students with attendance below 75% will be debarred from appearing in the End Semester Examinations. Medical leave must be supported by a certificate from the University Health Centre.

1.3 Grading System
The university follows a 10-point grading system:
- O (Outstanding): 91-100 marks → 10 grade points
- A+ (Excellent): 81-90 marks → 9 grade points
- A (Very Good): 71-80 marks → 8 grade points
- B+ (Good): 61-70 marks → 7 grade points
- B (Above Average): 51-60 marks → 6 grade points
- C (Average): 41-50 marks → 5 grade points
- F (Fail): Below 40 marks → 0 grade points

1.4 Backlog Examinations
Students who fail in any subject may appear for supplementary examinations held in June-July. A student can appear for a maximum of 3 attempts per subject. After 3 failures, the student must re-register for the course.

1.5 Malpractice
Any student found indulging in malpractice during examinations shall be awarded zero marks in that paper. Repeat offenders may face suspension from the university for up to two semesters.

CHAPTER 2: COURSE REGISTRATION

2.1 Registration Procedure
Students must register for courses at the beginning of each semester through the online portal. Late registration attracts a fine of Rs. 500 per week. Course registration closes two weeks after the commencement of classes.

2.2 Credit Requirements
- B.Tech programs: 160 credits minimum for graduation
- M.Tech programs: 80 credits minimum
- MBA programs: 120 credits minimum
- PhD programs: 16 coursework credits + thesis

2.3 Elective Courses
Each student must choose a minimum of 3 open electives from departments other than their own. This promotes interdisciplinary learning and broad academic exposure.

CHAPTER 3: PROJECT AND THESIS

3.1 Final Year Project
All B.Tech students must complete a final year project worth 12 credits. Projects must be registered by the 3rd week of the 7th semester. A project guide from the department will be allotted to each student or group.

3.2 Evaluation
Project evaluation consists of:
- Internal assessment by guide: 50 marks
- External viva-voce: 50 marks
- Written report submission: Mandatory (failing to submit results in incomplete grade)
