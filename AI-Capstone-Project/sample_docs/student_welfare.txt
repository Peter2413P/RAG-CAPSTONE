STUDENT WELFARE AND GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL HANDBOOK 2024-2025

PART A: STUDENT WELFARE SCHEMES

A.1 Health Care Services
The University Health Centre (UHC) provides:
- Free outpatient consultation by qualified doctors (Monday-Saturday, 8 AM to 8 PM)
- Emergency treatment 24/7
- Free generic medicines for common ailments
- Ambulance facility for emergencies
- First aid during university events and sports activities

A.1.1 Tie-up Hospitals
The university has MOU with the following hospitals for discounted treatment:
- City General Hospital: 30% discount on all services
- Apollo Outreach Clinic: Discounted dental and eye care
- Specialist referrals: University will arrange and partially reimburse

A.2 Counseling and Mental Health
The Student Counseling Centre (SCC) offers:
- Individual counseling sessions (free, confidential)
- Group therapy sessions for stress, anxiety, exam pressure
- Career counseling and guidance
- Peer support program trained by professional psychologists

PART B: GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL SYSTEM

B.1 Types of Grievances
The system handles complaints related to:
1. Academic matters (marks, attendance, examination)
2. Administrative issues (admissions, fees, records)
3. Hostel and mess complaints
4. Sexual harassment and gender issues
5. Ragging and bullying
6. Caste-based discrimination
7. Faculty misbehavior
8. Infrastructure and facilities

B.2 Grievance Submission Process
Step 1: Informal resolution – Approach HOD/Warden/faculty in-charge
Step 2: If unresolved, submit online grievance at: grievances.university.edu.in
Step 3: Grievance reaches concerned committee within 48 hours
Step 4: Hearing scheduled within 7 working days
Step 5: Resolution communicated within 15 working days

B.3 Internal Complaints Committee (ICC)
Handles all cases of sexual harassment as per POSH Act 2013:
- Chairperson: Senior female faculty member
- External member: NGO representative
- Two faculty members
- One student representative

Any complaint must be filed within 3 months of the incident. Strict confidentiality maintained.

B.4 SC/ST Cell
Dedicated cell for complaints related to caste-based discrimination:
- Liaison officer from SC/ST background
- Direct reporting to Vice Chancellor
- Cases resolved within 30 days

B.5 Anti-Ragging Committee
Zero tolerance policy. Any student found guilty of ragging faces:
- Immediate suspension
- Expulsion from hostel
- FIR lodged with police
- Cancellation of admission (repeat offenses)

B.6 Students' Union
Elected student body that:
- Represents student interests to administration
- Organizes cultural and technical fests
- Manages student clubs (50+ active clubs)
- Conducts elections every year under university guidelines

PART C: DISCIPLINARY ACTION

C.1 Minor Offenses (Fine of Rs. 500 to Rs. 2,000)
- Unauthorized entry into restricted areas
- Creating nuisance on campus
- Damaging university property (minor)

C.2 Major Offenses (Suspension / Rustication)
- Ragging
- Examination malpractice
- Physical assault
- Sexual harassment
- Drug/alcohol consumption on campus
- Submission of forged documents

C.3 Due Process
All disciplinary proceedings follow principles of natural justice:
- Show cause notice issued
- Student given opportunity to be heard
- Written order with reasons
- Right to appeal within 30 days
